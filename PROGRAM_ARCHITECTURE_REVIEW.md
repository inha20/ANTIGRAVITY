## The Question (Now Resolved)

**Original question:** Should repositories 1–3 be viewed as independent projects, or as empirical foundations of repository 4?

**Answer:** They are **empirical foundations of Paper 4 (Architecture B)**.

---

## Decision Record

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Decision | Architecture B: Empirical Foundation Model |
| Decision Date | 2026-06-05 |
| Decision Authority | Researcher (Choi Jonghun) + ANTIGRAVITY review |

### Rationale

1. **Conceptual connections are real and documented.** Papers 1–3 all contributed observations that became part of SRT.
2. **Historical development supports this reading.** The researcher moved from specific phenomena → general theory.
3. **Architecture B enables a unified research narrative** without sacrificing paper independence.
4. **Architecture A (independent) would misrepresent** the actual intellectual relationship between the papers.

### Papers 1–3 Remain Independent

Adopting Architecture B does NOT mean:
- Papers 1–3 are subsumed into Paper 4
- Papers 1–3 lose their independent publication paths
- Papers 1–3 are described as "chapters of Paper 4"

It DOES mean:
- Papers 1–3 are described as "empirical case studies" in their READMEs
- Paper 4 explicitly references Papers 1–3 as its empirical foundation
- The Research-Portfolio presents the program using Architecture B framing

## Implications for Future Work

1. **New papers** should be placed either as additional empirical cases (if documenting a new phenomenon) or as extensions of SRT (if developing theory).
2. **New repositories** must declare their role in Architecture B before being added.
3. **The theoretical hub role of Paper 4 is permanent** until a successor theoretical framework is developed.
